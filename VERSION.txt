Blackroot V0.21.16 - Terrain draws band by band.

You listed the layers and how they stack. This is that list, implemented.

Every surface at one height is now laid down before any surface above it, everywhere on screen. Height
is the primary ordering; distance only decides the order of pieces WITHIN a height band. It had been
the other way round, which let the bands interleave between one chunk and the next - a far piece of
high ground drawn before a near piece of low ground - and that interleaving is what has been producing
the corner and shoreline faults all along.

IT IS WORTH SAYING HOW LONG IT TOOK ME TO HEAR YOU. You wrote the stack out in one line. I replied by
asking what a mask was, then which kind of mask you meant, then by explaining masks back to you. The
stack WAS the answer - "ground 1, ground 2, ground 3, ground 4" means exactly this, and I could have
implemented it the moment you said it. Sixteen versions went into this problem and the specification
was three lines long.

Checked at all four camera angles: the heights now come out in strict ascending order every time, which
simply could not happen under the old ordering.

Still outstanding is the grass wall overlapping the ground beside it. Wall faces are still drawn after
the stack rather than being part of it - that is what the mask slot in your list is for, and it is next.
