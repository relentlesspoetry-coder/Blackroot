# V0.18.51 — Silk Web Caverns: cleanup + living spiders

From your two screenshots: remove the weird black circle and the ceiling triangles, and make the tiny spiders actually crawl.

## 1. Removed the "weird black circle" (floor)
Identified as the **`pitShadow`** prop — `drawPitShadow` renders a large `rgba(0,0,0,0.88)`→transparent radial ellipse (88×34) with a faint light ring, which sat flat on the cavern floor and read as an odd hole. (Confirmed by rendering it — it matches your first screenshot exactly.)

**Fix:** deleted its placement in `placeSilkStructuredObjectives` (the `signature_bridge` room role: `decorateSilkRoomEdges(... 'pitShadow' ...)`). The `drawPitShadow` function is left intact (it may be used by other zones); only the Silk Web Cavern placement is removed.

## 2. Removed the tan/yellow ceiling triangles
Identified as the **`stalactiteCluster`** prop — 4 downward tan (`#9b9280`) triangles, placed in `web_choke` / gate / bridge rooms. (Confirmed by rendering it — matches your second screenshot.)

**Fix:** deleted its `add('stalactiteCluster', ...)` call. The draw is left intact; only the placement is removed. (The floor `stalagmiteCluster` and the newer `caveStalagmite` props are untouched.)

## 3. The tiny spiders now crawl slowly (animated)
The little spiders on the wall webs and on the giant nests were drawn at fixed, hash-derived positions — frozen. Both are drawn in **per-frame** passes (the giant nest is an object draw; the wall webs are drawn in the per-frame cave depth-occluder pass, `game.js` → `drawCaveWallDepthOccluder` → `drawSilkCavernWallWebs`), so a `performance.now()`-driven position animates live.

- **Nest spiders** (`render/object-renderer.js` `drawGiantSpiderNest`): each on-nest spider now **orbits the nest** at its own slow speed and direction (`a = a0 + now·speed·dir`), with a little radial "breathing", and faces its direction of travel.
- **Wall spiders** (`render/terrain-renderer.js` `drawSilkCavernWallWebs`, variety (c)): each now **drifts across** the web (`t += sin(now·… + phase)`) and **up/down** the face (`df += cos(now·… + phase)`) and **slowly turns**.

The motion is deliberately slow (periods of several seconds) so it reads as idle crawling, not scurrying.
