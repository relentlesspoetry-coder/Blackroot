# V0.18.62 — Silk Depth / Occlusion Fixes (last of the request)

> "webs should not render over the top of the walls also the wall corners shouldnt render over the top of players, mobs, pets etc."

Showcase: [V0.18.62_wall_web_clip.png](V0.18.62_wall_web_clip.png) — wall webs now stop at the top edge (green line) and don't drape onto the floor; wall tops are clean.

## 1. Webs no longer render over the top of the walls
The user's screenshot showed a **tiered** cavern where the white silk **drape strands** hung down over everything — caking the flat **tops** of the lower walls/ledges and the **floor in front**.

Root cause: `drawSilkCavernWallWebs` (`render/terrain-renderer.js`) drew the silk sheet, drape strands, cross-weave, binding threads, corner orb webs, and the **variety-a "curtain"** (which deliberately hung `~32px` *past* the face bottom) with no clip — so silk spilled above the top edge and below the bottom edge.

**Fix:** each wall face's web is now **clipped to that face's quad** (`ctx.clip()` on `ta,tb,bb,ba` at the top of the per-face loop, closed with a matching `ctx.restore()`). Silk stays strictly on the vertical face — nothing over the wall top, nothing on the floor in front.

Verification (real function, reloaded code): rendering a face with a marked wall-top zone above and a floor zone below — **0 silk pixels** above the top edge, **0** below the bottom edge, ~39k on the face. The variety-a curtain (the worst offender) measures 0 spill.

## 2. Wall corners no longer render over players / mercs / pets / mobs
Tall cave walls (`elev 5` ≈ 140px) redraw themselves as a depth-sorted **occluder** so they can hide actors genuinely behind them. But the occluder over-reached and drew its corner/face over actors standing **at or in front of** a wall.

Two root-cause changes:
- **`caveWallOcclusionDepth`** (`render/terrain-renderer.js`): dropped the old `+ 0.38` forward bias. That bias pushed the wall's sort anchor ~0.38 depth-units **past its own frontmost top corner**, so it reached out over near-front actors. The bare front-corner anchor still covers everything behind the wall by a large margin, and stays rotation-robust (the frontmost corner is the `max` at any yaw).
- **`render` draw-order** (`game.js`): `caveOccluder` now ranks **1.9 — below `entity` (2)** (was `2.35`, above it). On an exact depth **tie** the actor now wins and draws on top, instead of the wall painting over it. Depth still dominates, so an actor genuinely behind a wall stays occluded; the occluder still ranks above `object`/`terrain`, so it keeps covering props/ground behind the wall.

Verification (faithful depth-sort model, `cos=1,sin=0`): behind-adjacent and NW actors → covered (correct); south/east/hugging-front and exact-tie actors → **not** covered (now visible). Reasoning holds across camera yaw because both the anchor (`max(corners)`) and entity depth are rotation-invariant.

### Honest scope note
Very tall walls still occlude actors that are **genuinely behind them in depth** even when those actors are only a tile or two away and "feel" like they're in the open — that is correct isometric occlusion, not a depth bug, and no scalar anchor changes it (confirmed by a screen-space raster: OLD vs a lower anchor rendered identically for those cases). If you want walls between the camera and your party to **fade / cut away** so the party is always visible, that's a separate feature — say the word and I'll add it.
