Dream Realms V0.13.50 — Seamless Water Rendering + Water Surface Chunk Cache Pass

SYSTEMS CHANGED
- render/terrain-renderer.js extends the existing terrain cache with water layers and owns water material, depth, shoreline, shimmer, invalidation, and cache statistics.
- game.js activates the shared ground/water cache before the existing structural terrain and world-entity passes.
- Existing editor tile/elevation invalidation and per-chunk terrain signatures now cover water type, elevation, and depth changes.
- Runtime, save, world, README, HTML, and version metadata identify V0.13.50.

SEAMLESS WATER
- Logical TILE.WATER cells remain unchanged. Only their visible top surfaces changed.
- Adjacent water tiles are rendered as one continuous top-down material field and affine-projected with the shared terrain chunks.
- Broad world-anchored blue-green fields cross tile and chunk boundaries without tile rims, bevels, checker tint, or repeated box textures.
- Shallow water uses lighter, calmer teal values; deep water uses darker attenuated values; cave and dungeon water use restrained underground palettes.
- Soft radial depth fields blend waterDepth differences without replacing the existing depth data.
- Shore tiles receive inward soft color fades while the existing structural deep-water faces continue to define blocked shorelines and vertical depth.
- Two-texel deterministic chunk overlap prevents raster cracks between adjacent projected water chunks.

ANIMATION / PERFORMANCE
- Each water elevation layer contains a cached base surface and a cached transparent shimmer surface.
- Wave bands are generated from world coordinates during chunk build, clipped to the water mask, and align across adjacent chunks.
- Runtime animation changes one global shimmer opacity phase per frame. It does not scan water tiles, allocate ripple objects, or rebuild chunks.
- Interior water tiles are excluded from the structural tile draw and depth-sort pass; only shoreline tiles retain structural depth work.
- The previous visible-water per-tile animation loop and unused per-tile water detail renderer were removed from the owning terrain module.
- Water shares the existing 16×16 chunks, 24-pixel material scale, camera culling, 72-entry bound, map identity, editor invalidation, and terrain signatures.
- waterDepth is included in signatures, so shallow/deep mutation rebuilds only affected chunks.

GAMEPLAY / SAVE COMPATIBILITY
- Collision, pathfinding, water blocking/swimming, fishing, spawn rejection, placement validation, combat distance, loot, NPC/mob placement, quests, dungeons, and saves continue to use logical water tiles.
- No volatile canvas, shimmer, cache, or signature state is serialized.
- Existing V0.13.49 and older saves load without migration.
- Ground chunks, walls, cliffs, props, actors, loot, nameplates, health bars, and chat bubbles retain their existing layering.
- Autonomous parties, bot pets, targeting, paper doll, inventory drops, item comparison, currency, Purify, resurrection, ranged attacks, Silk Web encounters, Dark Woods visuals, and Esc save/exit remain intact.
