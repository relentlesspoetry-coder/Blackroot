# V0.20.95 — Grass Cliff Faces No Longer Crush to Black

Reported with a screenshot: black patches in the terrain, on grass, at the edges of raised ground.

**They were not holes.** They were wall faces rendered at exactly `rgb(0,0,0)` — which on a cleared
canvas is indistinguishable from a hole. That is why the screenshot reads as missing geometry.

**And it was my regression, from V0.20.93.**

## Measured per material — west face, bottom of gradient

| material | side colour | side luma | bottom before fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| DEEP_GRASS | `#22381d` | 40 | **rgb(0,0,0)** |
| DARK_GRASS | `#1c2f1b` | 34 | **rgb(0,0,0)** |
| FOREST_FLOOR | `#33271d` | 40 | **rgb(0,0,0)** |
| UNDERBRUSH | `#1b3019` | 33 | **rgb(0,0,0)** |
| DIRT | `#543821` | 58 | 9 |
| STONE | `#5a584b` | 84 | 28 |
| RUIN | `#595644` | 81 | 25 |
| CAMP | `#6c4a2c` | 75 | 23 |

The four that failed are exactly the grass/forest family. Their side colours are dark enough that the
**pre-existing** `-42` bottom stop already sat at luma **2–5** — so the wall bottoms on grass cliffs have
been effectively black since long before this work. V0.20.93's directional offset of `-13.6` on the west
face consumed what little remained and took them to zero.

## Fix

Negative shading is scaled by the material's **headroom**:

```js
const headroom = Math.max(0.42, Math.min(1, parseLuma(top) / 70));
const S = amt => colorShade(top, amt < 0 ? amt * headroom : amt);
```

- **Stone, ruin, camp** compute headroom **1.0** → byte-identical output to before. The look I shipped in
  V0.20.93 for stone cliffs is preserved exactly.
- **Grass, underbrush, forest floor** get proportionally less darkening and lift clear of black.

This addresses my regression *and* the long-standing near-black, which is the better fix than simply
undoing V0.20.93 — the directional lighting was correct and worth keeping.

## Verified

| check | result |
|---|---|
| darkest material bottom | **0 → 6–9** luma |
| stone headroom | **1.0**, unchanged |
| perpendicular face contrast | **13–29** points — V0.20.93's directional lighting still reads |
| live black area, 4 grass-cliff locations × 3 camera yaws | **24,958 → 15,697 px, −37%** |

The live figure is an A/B against the unscaled version, not a before/after across page loads, so it
isolates this change.

## Honest notes

**The remaining black is real shadow, not zeroed pixels.** Tall grass walls are genuinely dark at their
base. I could lift them further, but that starts to look like flat paint rather than depth.

**The bigger win is still missing.** Nothing casts a shadow onto the ground at the foot of a cliff — four
attempts failed and are documented in V0.20.94. That would do more for how grounded this terrain reads
than any further tuning of the wall gradient itself.

## Sequence worth recording

Four consecutive versions on one visual complaint, each a genuinely different layer:

| version | layer | fault |
|---|---|---|
| V0.20.92 | geometry | 433 corner joins never emitted |
| V0.20.93 | shading | per-face direction computed, never applied |
| V0.20.94 | source data | generator floored high-frequency noise → 53× step density |
| **V0.20.95** | **shading, again** | **my own V0.20.93 crushing dark materials to black** |

The lesson from V0.20.93 was "an audit only finds the class of fault it is built to look for." V0.20.95
adds a second: **a fix in one layer can create a fault in that same layer for inputs you did not test.**
I verified the directional shading on stone and never checked grass.
