# Phase 9 — Dark Woods 360x360 Expansion: Old Ruins / Bandit Area

**Phase:** 9 of 28 (Dark Woods Zone Expansion + Resource Identity master plan)
**Previous version:** V0.17.47
**New version:** V0.17.48

---

## 1. Goal

Build Bandit's Fall: a ruined outpost occupied by bandits, with crumbling-wall/rubble/camp-clutter dressing
and 5 bandit enemy roles (Scout, Thug, Archer, Lookout, Leader), inside the `bandits_fall` region box defined
in Phase 3.

## 2. Audit First (Same Discipline as Phases 7–8)

Before writing anything, I audited what already existed: a fully-wired generic `bandit` enemy renderer,
animation profile, and sprite-bake/atlas target already existed in
`render/dark-woods-mob-procedural-model.js` and `systems/sprite-bake-system.js` — but zero actual bandit
stat data (`data/enemies.js`/`data/npcs.js`) had ever been written, so nothing bandit-shaped had ever
actually spawned. Most bandit-camp clutter props (crate, bedroll, fire, cookingSpit, weaponRack, brokenCart,
sparringDummy, supplyStack) already existed and were directly reusable; `ruinWall`/`ruinPillar` existed as
standing-only ruin props; a scaffolded-but-incomplete `barrel` prop existed (a shadow-size entry with no
actual draw function); and no archway, rubble pile, sack, rope, or barricade prop existed at all.

**A planning conflict was found and resolved before implementation**: the `bandits_fall` region box
(x:130–280, y:10–110) is not empty. It already contains roughly a dozen pre-existing, pre-Phase-3 mob camps,
roamers, and anchor points (ashroot horror camps, wisp patches, duskwisp anchors, a named rare) clustered in
the box's older (pre-resize) x:130–199 portion, all enforcing minimum camp spacing (18–20 tiles) against each
other. Building a full authored ruin-and-camp landmark there risked overlapping several of them. Since the
box's x:200–280 range was added by Phase 2's resize and is genuinely empty (confirmed — no existing camp or
anchor has x > ~198), the new Bandit's Fall landmark was built there instead (center `230,60`), leaving every
pre-existing spawn untouched. The older ruin remnants near x:137–178 (left standing by Phase 5 after removing
the Ashroot Hollow/Blackroot Catacombs cave entrances) were **not** touched or duplicated — they remain
general ruin-flavor dressing for the wider region, not the bandit outpost itself.

## 4. Root Cause / Implementation Notes

**Enemy family**: all 5 bandit roles use `rendererId: 'bandit'`/`mobVisualKey: 'bandit'` — the existing
generic renderer, differentiated by `visualScale` (Scout 0.94, Thug 1.1, Archer 0.98, Lookout 0.96, Leader
1.22) and `color` rather than separate art. Stats follow the same `MOB_DRAFTS` shape as every other Dark
Woods mob (`hp/attack/defense/speed/xp/gold/abilities/turnAbilities/aiProfile`), reusing existing `aiProfile`
values already recognized elsewhere in the file (`cave_ambusher` for Scout/Lookout, `charger` for Thug,
`ranged_caster` for Archer, `named_bruiser` for the Leader) rather than inventing new AI behavior. The Leader,
"Rurik the Fallen," follows the exact same named-rare pattern as `mob_ashroot_elder`/`spawn_dark_woods_named_ashroot_elder`
(`elite: true`, `rareNameplate: true`, its own single-mob spawn draft, placed via `DEFAULT_MOB_SPAWN_PLACEMENTS`
rather than the multi-count camp system). Loot tables were reused as-is (`loot_dark_woods_common_mobs` for
the 4 regular roles, `loot_dark_woods_named_rares` for the Leader) rather than adding new ones, since both
are already generic, shared tables used by multiple other mob families.

**Landmark layout** (`ensureBanditsFallLandmark`, center `230,60`): an outer wall ring built from `ruinWall`
placements around a rough rectangle (half-extents ~18×15 tiles), with a deliberate gap in the south wall
(no wall segment placed for `dx` between −4 and +4 at `dy +15`) flanked by two `barricade` props one row
inside the gap. A central ruined structure (`ruinArch` with `broken:true`, two `ruinPillar` with
`fallen:true` at different angles, one standing `ruinPillar`) sits near the middle. Two side pockets — a
partial `ruinWall` plus 2 `rubble` piles each — flank the structure, intended as ambush cover/line-of-sight
breaks per the plan's "side rubble pockets for ambushes." The bandit camp clutter (crates, barrels, a sack
pile, bedrolls, firepit, log seat, weapon rack, broken cart, rope bundle, target dummy) is spread through the
interior courtyard between the structure and the entry gap.

**A real placement bug was found and fixed before finalizing**: a standalone simulation of every `put(dx,dy,...)`
call (Section 5) initially showed the two flanking barricades at `(dx:-6,dy:15)`/`(dx:6,dy:15)` landing on
the *exact same coordinates* as two of the outer ring's own wall segments (the ring loop includes
`dx = ±6` at `dy = 15` specifically because those `dx` values are outside the `-4..4` gap range) — silently
overwriting 2 `ruinWall` placements with `barricade` instead of adding new props at new coordinates. Fixed by
moving the barricades to `dy:14` (one row further into the gap) instead of `dy:15`. Re-running the same
simulation after the fix confirmed 0 duplicate coordinates across all 46 placements.

**Collision**: `ruinArch` and `barricade` were added to the `solid` set (block movement, matching how
`ruinWall`/`ruinPillar` already work); `rubble`, `barrel`, `sackPile`, `ropeBundle` were added to `decorOnly`
(non-blocking ground clutter, matching crates/bedrolls/etc. already there). `ruinPillar`'s `fallen` variant
still blocks (it's already unconditionally in `solid`) — a toppled stone pillar is still a large obstacle,
consistent with the plan's "only large props block movement" rule.

## 5. Validation

Commands run:

```bash
find . -name "*.js" -print0 | xargs -0 -n1 node --check
find . -name "*.json" -print0 | xargs -0 -n1 python3 -m json.tool > /dev/null
```

**Results:** both passed with zero errors.

**Functional validation (executed in Node, not just traced):**

1. Loaded `data/npcs.js` directly and confirmed all 5 `mob_bandit_*` drafts exist and both new spawn drafts'
   `mobIds` resolve to real, existing mob draft ids — no dangling references.
2. Loaded the real `DARK_WOODS_MOB_CAMPS` array and computed the distance from the new `bandits_fall_camp`
   entry to every other camp: **nearest is `ashroot_blackened_stand` at 40.3 tiles**, comfortably clear of
   the required 20-tile `minCampSpacing` — confirming the "build in fresh territory" decision actually
   avoided the spacing conflict it was meant to avoid, not just in theory.
3. Loaded `data/default-zones.js` and ran the real region-lookup logic (same box/ring check validated in
   Phases 3–4) against the ruin center, camp, and named-leader coordinates — all three resolve to
   `bandits_fall`, confirming the landmark is genuinely inside its intended region.
4. Re-implemented every `put(dx,dy,type)` call from `ensureBanditsFallLandmark` in a standalone script and
   checked for duplicate output coordinates — found and fixed the barricade/wall-ring collision described in
   Section 4, then re-ran to confirm 46 placements land on 46 unique cells with zero overwrites, and
   confirmed the south entry gap (`dx -4..4` at `dy 15`) has no wall segment blocking it.
5. Confirmed both new loot table ids (`loot_dark_woods_common_mobs`, `loot_dark_woods_named_rares`) already
   exist in `data/loot-tables.js` — no dangling loot reference.

**Not validated (no browser available, same limitation as Phases 2–8):** the actual rendered appearance of
the ruin complex and camp clutter, whether the layout genuinely reads as "a real landmark" and "combat space
readable" to a player standing in it, and whether the 5 bandit enemies actually fight and animate correctly
in a live session.

## 7. Known Limitations

- All 5 bandit roles share one generic visual (differentiated only by scale/color) — no distinct silhouettes
  per role. This was a deliberate scope decision (the renderer already existed generically; building 5
  distinct character models was judged out of this phase's scope) but is worth flagging if visual variety
  turns out to matter more than expected once seen in-game.
- Only one bandit camp + one named leader were built, not multiple bandit pockets across the whole
  `bandits_fall` box — the box is large, and this phase prioritized one well-realized landmark over spreading
  thin. A future phase could add secondary bandit patrols/roamers if the area still feels sparse.
- The older ruin remnants near x:137–178 (Ashroot Hollow/Blackroot Catacombs leftovers) were left completely
  untouched — they're not part of the bandit outpost and weren't re-themed or connected to it.
- No in-browser visual/gameplay test was possible in this environment (see Section 5).
