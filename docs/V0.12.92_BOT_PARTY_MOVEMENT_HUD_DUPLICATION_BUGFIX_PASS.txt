Dream Realms V0.12.92 — Bot Party Movement HUD Duplication Bugfix Pass

Problem
- Bot players moved too fast and appeared to jump around.
- Party bots jittered beside the player while the player was idle.
- Each party bot also created a duplicate generic player HUD row showing Offline/Downed.

Root Cause
- followParty() returned false when a grouped bot was close to its formation point, so the bot immediately fell through into updateRoam() and kept trying to wander while still in the party.
- Bot IDs were stored both in botPartyMembers and partyMembers. getPartyRoster() rendered the real bot from botPartyMembers, then treated the same ID as a missing remote peer and rendered a fake Offline/Downed player row.

Fixes
- Party bots now always consume their party-follow update. They either move toward formation or hold position; they no longer fall through into autonomous roaming.
- Added idle formation dead-zone and velocity clearing to stop local jitter.
- Added deterministic per-bot formation offsets to reduce stacking.
- Reduced bot movement speed scalar and clamped bot movement deltas.
- Kept bot membership owned by botPartyMembers instead of duplicating bot IDs into partyMembers.
- Filtered legacy bot IDs out of partyMembers in normalization, party roster building, restore, and serialization.

Validation Targets
- Invite 1 bot: one bot HUD row, no Offline/Downed duplicate.
- Invite 4 bots: each bot appears once in the unified HUD.
- Stand still: bots hold nearby formation without jittering or wandering away.
- Move: bots follow at a controlled pace without large jumps.
- Save/load: bot party members still restore without duplicate HUD rows.
