Dream Realms V0.13.49 — Seamless Ground Rendering + Terrain Chunk Cache Pass

SYSTEMS CHANGED
- render/terrain-renderer.js now owns a bounded offscreen terrain chunk cache for walkable ground tops.
- game.js draws visible cached ground chunks before the existing structural terrain/object/entity pass and omits flat cached ground from per-tile sorting/drawing.
- systems/editor-system.js invalidates affected terrain chunks directly for tile paint and elevation edits.
- Runtime, save, world, README, HTML, and version metadata now identify V0.13.49.

SEAMLESS GROUND
- The logical map remains tile based. Only its visible ground-top presentation changed.
- Grass, underbrush, forest floor, dirt/camp, stone/ruin, cave floor, and Silk Web Cavern floor use distinct continuous material palettes.
- Broad world-anchored tonal patches cross tile and chunk boundaries, while irregular material-edge patches soften grass/dirt/stone/cave transitions.
- Sparse grass blades, earth marks, stone cracks, cave wear, and Silk Web residue are baked into chunks once rather than recreated each frame.
- Walkable cached ground no longer draws individual diamond rims, bevels, checker tint, or floor lips that exposed the gameplay grid.
- Water, water depth, cave walls, cliffs, and elevation faces remain structurally explicit for gameplay readability.

CHUNK CACHE / PERFORMANCE
- Terrain uses 16×16 logical chunks with compact 24-pixel-per-tile material surfaces and three-tile build padding.
- Chunks are built lazily for the visible camera area, retained in a 72-entry bounded cache, and reused while the player or camera moves.
- World-space source textures are affine-projected at draw time, so zoom and continuous camera yaw do not trigger rebuilds or lose alignment.
- Per-chunk terrain signatures detect type/elevation mutation and rebuild only changed chunks; neighboring signature coverage preserves softened edges.
- Editor tile and elevation tools also invalidate the affected chunk region immediately.
- Flat cached ground is excluded from the old per-tile ground draw and depth-sort work. Structural terrain still uses the existing owning pass.
- Unsupported offscreen-canvas environments retain the existing per-tile terrain renderer as a safe fallback.

GAMEPLAY / COMPATIBILITY
- Collision, pathfinding, spawn/placement validation, combat distance, ranged attacks, loot placement, dungeon reachability, quests, and save/load still use the original tile data.
- Cache surfaces and signatures are transient runtime state and are not written to saves.
- Existing V0.13.47 and older saves remain compatible without migration.
- Actor/object/effect/UI layering is unchanged: ground remains below props, loot, characters, health bars, nameplates, and chat bubbles.
- Autonomous parties, bot pets, targeting, paper doll, inventory drops, item compare, currency, Purify, resurrection, dungeon encounters, Dark Woods visuals, and Esc save/exit remain intact.
