## The two hard problems (from the Phase 18/22 audits)

1. **Silk Web Cavern had ZERO water tiles.** Its "pools" (`venomPool`, `waterSeep`) are `visualOnly`
   dressing *objects*, not `TILE.WATER`. Fishing requires standing next to real water tiles.
2. **Fishing actively disables itself in dungeons** (`render`/aquatic-`update` early-return on
   `currentZone==='dungeon'`; fishing's own `zoneKey` returned `'dark_woods'` for dungeons).

## Implementation

### Carve real cave pools (`systems/dungeon-system.js`)
New `carveSilkWebFishingPools(map, objects, rooms, floor)` converts small ellipses of **existing carved
`CAVE_FLOOR`** to `TILE.WATER` (with `waterDepth`) at deterministic offsets from the fixed
`SILK_WEB_FLOOR_CONFIG` rooms — so pools never punch through walls/corridors and their positions (and
thus respawn/depletion keys) stay stable across runs. Called in `generateSilkWebCavernFloor` **before**
reachability + enemy placement (water is non-walkable; enemies avoid it). Pools: floor 1 one shallow
(depth 5); floor 2 shallow (5) + deeper (7); floor 3 mid (6) + **deep (10)** for the deep-water-only
Broodpool Angler.

### Make fishing dungeon-aware (`systems/fishing-system.js`)
- `zoneKey()` returns `'silk_web_cavern'` for that dungeon (other dungeons → `'dungeon'`, no table → no
  fishing).
- `activeMap`/`activeObjects` resolve `game.dungeonMap`/`game.dungeonObjects` for the silk-web zone.
- Added `FISH_TABLES.silk_web_cavern` (3 fish + junk), with `levelRequired`, and `deepWaterOnly` +
  `rareNodeXpBonus` on Broodpool.
- `render()` now draws the fishing line inside the Silk Web dungeon (other dungeons still excluded;
  ambient swimming fish stay overworld/cave only, since the carved pools are small).

The Phase 22 `eligibleFish` level + deep-water gate and the Phase 19 first-discovery/rare-bonus wiring
apply automatically once the zone/table resolve. Zone-exclusivity is automatic (silk fish only live in
`FISH_TABLES.silk_web_cavern`, resolved only inside the Silk Web dungeon).

## Pre-existing bug fixed (discovered during verification)
`render/entity-renderer.js` `drawFishingActionOverlay(d, screen, scale)` referenced an **undeclared `s`**
(a rename that missed two lines) instead of the `screen` parameter, throwing `ReferenceError: s is not
defined` for **every** fishing player render (overworld and dungeon) — caught by the render-fault guard
(no crash) but spamming errors, and the accurate rod-tip anchor was never published (the fishing line
fell back to a world-space approximation). Fixed `s?.x`/`s?.y` → `screen?.x`/`screen?.y`. Unrelated to
the cave fish; surfaced by the Phase 23 dungeon-fishing verification. Verified: 5 direct calls, 0 throws,
rod-tip anchor now published.

## Known limitations
- Broodpool Angler requires a "deep/rare" cast (the existing free-cast makes deep water rare ~12% of the
  time, same as overworld deep-water rare fish), so it is intentionally very rare even at the deep pool.
- Ambient swimming fish are not rendered in the small dungeon pools (cosmetic only); casting/catching work.
- Water rendering inside a cave/dungeon context has not been visually confirmed in a live render (verified
  functionally); it is listed as a manual test.
- Fishing has no depletion/respawn model (fish-anywhere); the plan's per-fish respawn is a Phase 24 item.
