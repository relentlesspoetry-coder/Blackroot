Dream Realms V0.12.3 — Deep Water System Pass

Scope:
- Diagnose and fix shallow water behavior.
- Generate and normalize real water depths from 5 to 12 blocks.
- Support vertical swimming through the full water column.
- Add breath/oxygen tracking while underwater.
- Improve visual depth cues with darker water centers, light attenuation, deep shafts, and underwater overlays.

Diagnosis:
- V0.12.1/V0.12.2 stored waterDepth on many water tiles and rendered edge shafts, but gameplay still treated water mostly as a boolean swimming tile.
- Player swimming used underwater true/false instead of an actual depth coordinate.
- Older generated maps/saves could still contain water without consistent depth metadata.
- Cave pools created per-tile random depths but were not normalized as connected bodies.

Implementation:
- Added DR.normalizeWaterDepthsForMap(map) in systems/world-system.js.
- Overworld generation now normalizes connected water bodies after terrain and landmark placement.
- Cave floor generation now normalizes water pools after cave decoration.
- systems/swimming-system.js now owns vertical swimDepth, max water depth checks, breath tracking, underwater dungeon interaction state, and depth overlays.
- render/terrain-renderer.js now uses waterDepth for stronger darkening, attenuation, and deep-water cues.

Controls:
- Enter water to swim.
- Hold U to dive downward.
- Hold Space to ascend and surface.
- Press E near an underwater entrance while underwater to enter it.

No cascade patching:
- Changes were made directly in world-system.js, cave-system.js, swimming-system.js, terrain-renderer.js, game.js, and player.js ownership points.
