# V0.20.77 — Front View: the Rider's Lower Body Goes Behind the Mount

Reported: *"Let's finish the front view completely first. The lower body should be hidden behind the
mount's head and upper body."*

## Measured before changing anything

An ASCII colour map of the live canvas (now possible — see V0.20.76 on the transform fix) showed the
answer immediately:

| | dy |
|---|---|
| rider's lower body — a solid white block | **−58..−31** |
| rider hip / foot | −51 / −26 |
| overlay band ended at | **−53** |

The band stopped **exactly where the lower body begins**. It was never a layering or ordering problem;
the band was simply two pixels short of the thing it needed to cover.

## The fix

Facing the camera, the band now runs from the head down past the rider's legs:

```js
const riderFootY  = Math.round(oy - saddle + 28);
const bandBottom  = Math.max(shoulderY, Math.min(bottom, riderFootY + 4));
```

so the animal's head **and** upper body do the occluding. Box goes from **−86..−53** to **−86..−19**.

**Extending is safe by construction.** The band only clips a *redraw of the mount*, so it can paint the
animal's own chest and can never reveal background — the property that made the V0.20.72 cut-out approach
too dangerous and this one not.

## Width is taken from the head and chest rows only

The first attempt measured min/max across the whole band and produced a **152px** box that buried the
rider's legs as well — the very thing V0.20.72 splayed them to ±31 to avoid.

Cause, measured on the black wolf at southeast:

| dy | width | |
|---|---|---|
| −59 | 65 | chest appears |
| −56 | 81 | |
| **−53** | **142** | the angled flank sweeps out to l = −87 |

Sampling the full band inherited that flank. Restricting the width measurement to head + withers
(`shoulderY + 4`) gives **90px**, which covers the lower body (−15..+16) while leaving the boots outside.

## A clamp that was built and then removed

I added a clamp limiting the spread to the head span plus a proportional margin, and asserted in a
comment that it was what kept the boars in check. Then I measured it: **byte-identical widths on all 11
mounts.** Completely inert. Removed — this is the same dead-complexity-with-a-false-explanation pattern
deleted in V0.20.75, and it does not get to ship twice.

## Known, and deliberately not touched

The boars get much wider front boxes — **briar boar 141px, old tusk 202px** — because the shoulder
detection **never fires** for them:

| mount | head-width probe | threshold (×1.9) | actual max width | fires? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| black wolf | 26 | 49 | 143 | **yes**, at −59 |
| briar boar | 82 | 156 | 131 | no |
| old tusk | 106 | 201 | 182 | no |

Their skull merges straight into the shoulders, so there is no narrow-then-widen transition to find, and
`shoulderY` falls back to 42% of height.

**This is pre-existing, not introduced here.** For a boar, covering that width may well be correct — the
rider's legs sit behind its bulk anyway. Left alone rather than "fixed" blind; it needs eyes on a boar
mount first.
