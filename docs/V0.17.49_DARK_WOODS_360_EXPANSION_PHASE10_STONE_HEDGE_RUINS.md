## 3. Root Cause / Implementation Notes

**Deliberately new visual identity.** Before writing anything, I checked for existing stone/megalith props
and found none (`ruinWall`/`ruinPillar` are the only existing stone-ruin props, and both are now strongly
associated with the brown/tan bandit-masonry look from Phase 9). Since this phase's own validation criteria
require the result to be "visually distinct from bandit ruins," 4 new props were built with a deliberately
different grey-blue mossy-megalith palette rather than reusing or re-tinting the bandit ruin props:
`standingStone` (a tall leaning menhir, with optional `mossy` and glowing `rune` overlay flags),
`runeStone` (a smaller flat-topped stone with a pulsing glow and carved-symbol strokes), `brokenSlab` (a
single cracked, fallen flat stone — distinct from Phase 9's `rubble`, which is a scattered multi-chunk pile),
and `magicResidue` (a few small rising, fading glow motes plus a faint ground haze — the "magical residue"
atmosphere called for by the plan, done as an animated prop rather than a new terrain-tile system, since a
terrain-level effect is explicitly Phase 13's scope).

**Placement location and why.** The region box (`x:130–250, y:190–300`) sits entirely in the fresh territory
added by Phase 2's resize, and Phase 4 already carved a road spur ending at `(200,240)` specifically to reach
this region. The landmark center was placed at `(210,250)` — about 14 tiles from that road endpoint — and
checked against every other landmark placed so far: 41–52 tiles from the two Phase 4 lanterns at
`(196,200)`/`(220,210)`, and 186 tiles from the Phase 9 Bandit's Fall camp. No conflicts.

**Geometry, not noise.** Unlike the seeded-noise scatter dressing used in Phases 6/8 (approach/concealment
dressing, where organic randomness fits the theme), a stone circle calls for precise, regular geometric
placement. `ensureStoneHedgeRuinsLandmark` computes 12 evenly-spaced angles around the center at radius 9 and
places a `standingStone` (or, at every 5th position, a fallen `brokenSlab` instead) at each — except a
90-degree arc is skipped entirely to create the "partial ring" the plan calls for. That gap's center angle
was deliberately set to exactly match the road's actual approach direction: the road endpoint `(200,240)`
sits at almost precisely −135° from the landmark center, and the gap was centered on −135° for exactly that
reason (verified, not assumed — see Section 4). Five `runeStone`s sit on an inner radius-5 ring, and 3
`magicResidue` motes are scattered near radius 4–5.5, all safely outside the ring's own outermost extent so
none of it visually crowds the true center.

**The center stays clear by construction, not by a separate check.** No prop in this phase is placed closer
than radius 5 to the landmark center — verified directly (Section 4) rather than assumed, since Phase 9
already taught the lesson that authored-geometry placements can silently collide if not checked.

**Collision:** `standingStone` was added to `solid` (a tall upright megalith is a large blocking prop, same
logic as `ruinWall`/`ruinPillar`/`tree`). `brokenSlab`, `runeStone`, and `magicResidue` were added to
`decorOnly` (flat/small/ethereal — none of them should block movement, matching the plan's "only large props
block" rule).

## 4. Validation

Commands run:

```bash
find . -name "*.js" -print0 | xargs -0 -n1 node --check
find . -name "*.json" -print0 | xargs -0 -n1 python3 -m json.tool > /dev/null
```

**Results:** both passed with zero errors.

**Functional validation (executed in Node, not just traced)** — learning directly from Phase 9's caught bug,
every placement call in `ensureStoneHedgeRuinsLandmark` was re-implemented in a standalone script and
checked before considering the phase done:

```
Total placements: 19, Unique cells: 19, Duplicates: 0
Ring stones placed: 8 of 12 positions (4 skipped for the gap - confirms a genuine "partial" ring)
Minimum distance from center among all placements: 5.00 tiles
Any placement within radius 4 of center: false
Dist from lantern (196,200) to landmark center: 51.9 tiles
Dist from lantern (220,210) to landmark center: 41.2 tiles
Dist from Bandit's Fall camp to landmark center: 186.1 tiles
Road approach angle from center: -135.0 degrees
Gap center angle: -135.0 degrees - match: true
```

This directly confirms: no accidental overlapping placements (the exact class of bug found in Phase 9), the
center is genuinely clear for Phase 11's waypoint, the landmark doesn't crowd any other landmark placed so
far, and the ring's gap is precisely — not approximately — aligned with the road that leads to it.

**Not validated (no browser available, same limitation as Phases 2–9):** the actual rendered appearance of
the stone ring, glow effects, and whether it visually reads as intended once seen — including whether it is
genuinely distinguishable from the bandit ruins at a glance, which is a visual judgment this environment
cannot make.

## 6. Known Limitations

- No waypoint, wisps, or combat space tuning were added — entirely deferred to Phase 11 by design.
- "Magical residue terrain" was implemented as an animated prop, not a terrain-tile-level effect; a true
  terrain overlay (if desired later) is Phase 13 scope.
- The ring's exact stone count/spacing/gap width are a first-pass aesthetic choice and may need visual
  tuning once actually seen rendered.
- No in-browser visual test was possible in this environment (see Section 4).
