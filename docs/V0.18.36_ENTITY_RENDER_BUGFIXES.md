# V0.18.36 ‚Äî Entity render & identity bug fixes

Three reported bugs (with screenshots), each fixed at the root cause in its owning module. No gameplay, art, data, or other systems were changed.

---

## Bug 1 ‚Äî Rotling snare roots STILL render at the player's waist, not the feet
**Report:** *"the rotlings roots spell still doesn't render at the players, bots, merc or pets feet when rooted."*

**Why the V0.18.34 fix didn't land it:** V0.18.34 lowered the *tendril heights* inside `drawRotlingRootEntangleEffect`, but the problem is the **anchor**, not the tendrils. The effect anchors to the target's `_rootVfxFootAnchor` (set in `render/entity-renderer.js` to `visualFoot.y + 5`). For **humanoids** ‚Äî players, bots, mercs, NPCs drawn via `render/humanoid-base-renderer.js` ‚Äî that point is **~39px above the actual rendered feet**:

- The humanoid rig's `ground` anchor is authored at the **body origin** (`y + 0`), and `_rootVfxFootAnchor` is derived from that origin.
- But the legs actually draw the **boots at `foot.y ‚âà +38..44`** below the origin.
- **Measured in-browser** (fighter/bard/cleric, origin `screenY = 200`): boot bottom at `y = 244`, old anchor at `y = 205` ‚Üí a **39px gap** landing the roots at the hips/waist.

Mobs/enemies use their own models that draw feet *at* `visualFoot`, so `_rootVfxFootAnchor` is already correct for them ‚Äî this only affected humanoids.

**Fix (root cause, isolated):**
- `render/humanoid-base-renderer.js` ‚Äî `draw()` now publishes the **true rendered foot line** in `_lastGroundAnchor.groundY` = `y + max(near-leg foot.y, far-leg foot.y)` (all four rig variants ‚Äî standing/sitting/swim/meditate ‚Äî populate `legs.near/far.foot`).
- `render/effects-renderer.js` ‚Äî `drawRotlingRootEntangleEffect` **prefers `target._lastGroundAnchor.groundY`** when present; otherwise it keeps the old `_rootVfxFootAnchor ‚Üí _groundContactAnchor ‚Üí _spriteFootAnchor` chain (so mobs are unchanged).
- `_lastGroundAnchor` had **no other consumers** (verified), so this is scoped entirely to the root-entangle VFX.

**Verified in-browser after the fix:** the anchor moved from **39px above the feet ‚Üí 6px from the boots** (238 vs boot-bottom 244) across fighter, bard, and cleric. The effect's own `+4` ground offset then puts the roots right at boot level.

## Bug 2 ‚Äî A living boar was named "Corpse Bag (2)"
**Report:** *"Weird bug where a boar was named death bag, like it was the loot bag from a corpse."*

**Root cause:** When several corpses drop near each other, `mergeCorpseLootIntoBag` (`systems/loot-system.js`) merges their loot into one corpse and **renames that corpse `Corpse Bag (N)`** (plus sets corpse-loot flags). Overworld mobs self-respawn via `Enemy.respawn()` (`entities/enemy.js`), which **only reset combat state** (position, hp, alive, threat, patrol). So the same pooled enemy object came back **alive still named "Corpse Bag (2)"**, with a threat bar and a normal boar body ‚Äî a live boar wearing a loot-bag's identity.

**Fix:** `Enemy.respawn()` now restores the real identity and clears all corpse/loot-bag state:
- `this.name = this.baseType.name` (the original mob name; `baseType` is never renamed).
- clears `corpseLoot`, `isLootCorpse`, `corpseLooted`, `corpseLootInteractPending`, `corpseVisualExpired`, `corpseLootSummary`, `corpseLootCreatedAt`, `corpseLootZoneId`, `groupLootMergedInto`, `blocksMovement`.
- removes itself from `game.lootableCorpses`.

## Bug 3 ‚Äî NPCs/companions render over the player
**Report:** *"NPCs shouldn't render over the player layer, unless the player is behind them."*

**Root cause:** Every entity (player, bots, mercs, pets, NPCs, enemies) is queued into the depth-sorted world pass at the **same bias**: `depthFast(x, y, elev) + 0.95` (`game.js`). `depthFast` is purely foot-position based, so a companion/NPC sharing the player's exact tile ties on depth and the sort falls back to array order ‚Äî the later-pushed actor paints over the player.

**Fix (`game.js`):** the player gets a small **`+0.05` depth nudge** so it wins same-tile and hair's-breadth ties. The nudge is a small fraction of a single tile's depth step, so an actor **clearly in front of the player (> ~0.1 tile)** still has more depth and correctly occludes it ‚Äî i.e. "unless the player is behind them" still reads correctly.
