# V0.20.37 — Reduce Motion (Roadmap Item 20)

The **2nd of 12** accessibility features, extending the screen-shake toggle (V0.20.19).

## The feature

A single **Reduce Motion** pref — off by default, its own row in the **Accessibility** settings
section, persisted through `uiPrefs`. When ON:

1. **Zeroes screen shake** — a superset of `reduceScreenShake`, at the same one consumer gate
   (`getCameraProjectionCache`).
2. **Thins decorative particle density by half** — via `effectQualityScale()`, the single funnel every
   mote/spark/wisp count already passes through (`count × effectQualityScale`).

## The spec's hard constraint, honoured by construction

The spec requires reduced motion to *"preserve gameplay timing and information."* This does so **by
construction** — it only touches:

- the **count** of decorative particles, and
- the **screen shake**.

It never touches an effect's lifetime, radius, damage timing, or existence. So **every ring, bolt,
telegraph, health bar and damage number still draws exactly as before** — there's just less swirling
motion and no camera shake. Verified directly (a gameplay ring still spawns and draws with the pref
on).

## Design decisions (the V0.20.19 pattern)

| decision | why |
|---|---|
| **off by default** | a default must never silently change how the game looks for someone who didn't ask |
| gates at the **read/consumer**, not the producers | shake still accrues and effects still request their full particle counts; only the read is reduced — instantly reversible mid-session, no state to reconcile |
| its **own toggle** in Accessibility | someone looking for it will look for the words "reduce motion" |
| **superset of screen shake** for the shake gate | reduced motion sensibly includes no-shake, without forcing the user to toggle both |

## Note

This is a **v1** reduced-motion: shake + decorative particle density, the two cleanest high-impact
funnels. A fuller pass could also calm animated fog drift and `hitFlash` pulsing — but those are
smaller motion sources and each needs its own consumer hook, so they're a follow-up rather than part of
this bounded, verified change.
