# V0.20.72 — The Mount's Head Draws Over the Rider

Reported: *"hide the player's lower body from this angle and only show their legs on each side of the
mount's head. Their lower body is blocking the mount's head."*

## Two approaches — one rejected on inspection

### Rejected: cut a hole in the rider

The literal reading. Clip the rider with an even-odd hole across the head box so the head shows through.
Built it, then worked out what it would produce:

1. **The head's silhouette is rounded; a clip hole is a rectangle.** Wherever the head did not fill the
   corners, the player would see **the ground straight through the character's chest.**
2. **The hole lands on the wrong body part.** Measured, the head box spans **y −91 to −54** — the rider's
   *torso*. The legs sit at **y −27**, well below it. "Hide the lower body" would have cut a window in
   the chest and left the legs untouched.

Both only became visible by measuring the actual geometry rather than trusting the description.

### Shipped: draw the head over the top

The mount is drawn a second time **after** the rider, clipped to the measured head box. Painting can only
ever *add* the animal's own pixels — it can never reveal background, which is precisely the failure the
cut-out risked.

Plus `buildRidingRig` now splays the legs wider in front views:

| | side/rear | **front** |
|---|---|---|
| knee | 17 | **27** |
| foot | 19 | **31** |

So the legs sit either side of the head rather than in front of it. Side and rear keep the narrow seat,
where a wide splay would just look bow-legged.

## The test harness was broken — and I proved it rather than assuming

V0.20.71 shipped admitting the layering was unproven. The reason turned out to be the measurement, not
the code.

**The canvas is `{alpha: false}`.** Every pixel always reports alpha 255, so the
"count pixels with alpha > 0" metric returned **100% regardless of what was drawn**. It measured nothing.

Re-tested properly — fill the canvas magenta, re-render, count remaining magenta:

```
magentaAfterFill:   14400
magentaAfterRender: 0        → render() DOES repaint on demand
```

So a frozen-time A/B is valid in principle. A later `overlayCalls: 0` also turned out to be **my test's
leftover state** — the character was not actually mounted that run — not a code fault.

## What is still not proven

A clean before/after image of the whole scene. Effects use `Math.random` per frame, so two renders never
match pixel-for-pixel regardless of this change — a full-scene diff cannot isolate one overlay. The
geometry and call path are confirmed; **how it looks is the user's call.**

If the head still hides, the next thing to check is whether the head box (measured from a `south`-facing
probe) matches the pose actually drawn in a `front` view — the box is currently off-centre at x −11.5,
which may mean the probe and the live draw disagree about where the head is.
