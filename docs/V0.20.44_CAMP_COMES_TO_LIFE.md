# V0.20.44 — The Camp Comes to Life

A side request: the Dead Lantern camp NPCs stood frozen at their posts; the few that moved "teleported"
around. Give them idle + walking animation, and have some retire into tents at night. Make the camp feel
alive.

## What was actually wrong

The animation tech already existed — NPCs render through a baked multi-frame atlas whose action is keyed
by `getEntityAtlasAction`, which returns `'walk'` when `moveBlend > 0.08` and `'idle'` otherwise, and the
live standing puppet has idle `breathe` + a full walk cycle. The problems were **behavior**:

1. **Only 2 of 28** camp NPCs ever moved (the two with authored `patrol`), in a slow radius-2 clockwork orbit.
2. **The "teleport" look**: `moveBlend` was raised on a successful step but **decayed the instant a step
   was blocked** — and a dense camp blocks constantly — so the walk animation never sustained. The NPC
   slid its position while showing a frozen pose.
3. **No night behavior** existed at all.

## The design (per the requested steer)

- **Bustling by day**, **almost everyone sleeps at night**.

Every overworld camp NPC now runs a home-anchored two-state stroll: walk to a point near its post, pause
a beat, pick another. Essential NPCs (quest/trainer/vendor) keep a tighter leash (radius 1.7) so they
stay findable; ambient folk roam wider (2.7). At night almost everyone walks to the nearest tent and
sleeps; the ethereal dream spirit keeps its vigil. At dawn they wake and return.

## The key fix

`moveBlend` is now the NPC's walking **intent**, set from its state (walking vs paused/idle) in
`updateNpcPatrols`, **not** from per-frame step success in `_stepNpcToward`. So a blocked frame in the
crowd keeps the gait (the NPC "walks in place" against the crowd for a frame) instead of snapping to
idle. That single change is what makes the walk animation actually engage.

**Sleeping** is a runtime `_asleep` flag, honored in four places so a sleeper truly vanishes: the NPC
render loop, both interaction pickers (`findNearbyNpc`, `findNpcAtWorld`), and the minimap.

## Item 16 safety (kept by construction)

- **Home anchored to the authored post** (`CAMP_NPC_LAYOUT`), not the runtime position — a save taken
  mid-stroll can't drift the camp on reload.
- `isWalkable` gates every step; a step overlapping another awake NPC or the player is refused.
- The NPC being talked to holds still.
- NPCs stationed away from camp (bandit fence, silk-web quartermaster) keep their posts — they never
  march to a camp tent.
- All state is runtime; sleep re-derives from the world clock on reload — nothing to save or migrate.

## Verified — live, through the real game loop

The first pass had two bugs the real-path check caught: **0/180 frames had anyone walking** (the decay
bug above) and **5 NPCs couldn't reach a congested tent**. After the intent-based `moveBlend` +
looser pass spacing + a wider sleep-arrival radius:

| check | result |
|---|---|
| day: frames with someone walking | **120/120** (was 0/180), ~4 strolling at once |
| night: camp NPCs asleep | **26/27** (only the ethereal Spirit Healer up) |
| dawn: still asleep | **0** |
| NPCs on a blocked tile | **0** |
| homes anchored to authored posts | **yes** |
| console errors | **0** |
| **live game loop** (20s real deep-night) | 26/27 slept in tents on their own ✅ |

A live probe confirmed the figures still visible at night are **autonomous adventurer bots**
(`kind: 'bot'`), a separate population — correctly unaffected.

## Honest note

The wandering adventurer **bots** don't sleep — they're simulated other players, a different system, and
making them turn in would be out of scope and risk their AI. So the camp *staff* empty into tents at
night while the odd adventurer still passes through, which reads fine. If you want the bots to thin out
at night too, that's a separate, larger change to the bot population system.
