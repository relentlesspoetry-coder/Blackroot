# V0.18.63 — Wall Cut-Away & Bard 5-Song Cap

> "walls still cover the player, bots. merc and pets at different angles. Also the bards songs are limited to 2 active songs at once it should be 5 active songs."

Showcase: [V0.18.63_wall_cutaway_before_after.png](V0.18.63_wall_cutaway_before_after.png) — top: two party members hidden behind a wall; bottom: same scene, walls cut away, both visible.

## 1. Walls still rendered over the party → cut-away
V0.18.62 reduced the occluder's over-reach and let actors win depth **ties**, but that only fixed the razor-thin tie band. Tall cave walls (`elev 5` ≈ **140px**) still drew over the player/mercs/pets/bots, because those actors are genuinely at **lower depth** than a wall standing in front of them — that's correct isometric occlusion, not a depth bug (I confirmed with a screen-space raster that no scalar anchor changes it). The user wants the party visible regardless.

**Fix — cut-away.** New `computeCaveWallCutaways()` (`render/terrain-renderer.js`) runs each frame and, for every **friendly** actor (player, mercs, pets, bots), marks the cave-wall tiles whose tall body would draw over that actor:
- wall is **in front** of the actor (greater camera depth), and
- within the wall's tall-body **screen reach** (≈5 tiles of depth ≈ 140px up), and
- **horizontally overlaps** the actor (tile-proxy-x within ~1.2).

It's **projection-based** (uses the live camera `cos/sin`), so it tracks the camera-front walls at **every yaw**. `drawCaveWallDepthOccluder` early-returns for a marked tile — it **skips the occluder redraw**, so the wall is painted only by the **base terrain pass** (which draws it *behind* the actors). The party draws in front of the wall and stays fully visible; the wall is still there. **Enemies are not cut away**, so they stay hidden behind walls.

Wired in `game.js render()`: `this._caveWallCutaways = this.computeCaveWallCutaways()` just before the world draw loop.

**Verification:**
- Before/after render (showcase): party members hidden behind a wall become visible with the cut-away, the wall still drawn behind them.
- Yaw sweep `0/45/90/135/180/270°` on a synthetic scene: the cut set contains exactly the camera-**front** adjacent/overlapping walls at each angle, and never the behind or far walls — e.g. at 0° it cut the south/east adjacent walls; at 180° (camera flipped) it cut the north walls instead.

### Why cut-away (skip) instead of fade
Skipping the occluder is cheap, robust, and literally "walls don't cover the party." The trade-off: a party member walking *behind* a wall shows through it (x-ray) — usually fine since the party is in your room. If you'd prefer walls to go **translucent** instead of fully cut, that's a follow-up (needs group-alpha compositing); say the word.

## 2. Bard songs: 2 → 5 active
`applyBardMajorSongLimit` (`systems/spell-system.js`) pruned the oldest major song from the same bard once **2** were active. Raised the cap to **5** (`MAX_ACTIVE_MAJOR_SONGS`). 12 bard spells carry `majorSong`, so the cap applies across the kit. (This is the only song-concurrency cap — no separate UI/config slot limit exists.)
