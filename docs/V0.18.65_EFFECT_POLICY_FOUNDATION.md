## Recon before building
I surveyed the codebase against the roadmap's foundation items and found:

| Roadmap foundation | Status in codebase |
|---|---|
| **Item 9 — data-driven content architecture** | **Largely already present.** `core/registry.js` is a read-only descriptor/reference-validation layer over the data-owned `DR.*_BY_ID` catalogs (item/spell/quest/npc/recipe/…), with `runBootValidation`, `has/get/hasByName`, `auditRuntimeReferences`, compiler-error recording, and a `DESCRIPTOR_VERSION`. `core/ids.js` canonicalizes ID formats. The hard-coded Bard song cap (V0.18.63) was the symptom — and the roadmap's own cautionary example. |

So the foundation work targets the real gap: **Item 13**.

## What changed
### 1. `core/effect-policy.js` (new) — `window.DreamRealms.effectPolicy`
One authoritative, **data-driven** policy. A `CATEGORIES` table maps a category → its rules:

| field | meaning |
|---|---|
| `scope` | `target` (counted across all sources) / `sourceAndTarget` (per source→target pair) |
| `maxConcurrent` | how many may be active at once (0 = unlimited) |
| `overflow` | `dropOldest` / `rejectNew` |
| `stackMode` | `refresh` / `stack` / `replaceStronger` / `independent` |
| `cooldownGroup` | shared consumable-cooldown key (matches `item.cooldownGroup`) |
| `dispelType` | `magic` / `poison` / `disease` / `curse` / `none` |

Query API: `ruleFor` · `limitFor` · `cooldownGroupFor` · `categoryOf` · `enforceConcurrency`, plus a non-fatal boot `validate()` (same spirit as `registry.js`). **Ownership:** it owns the policy catalog only — it applies no effects and stores no per-actor state; the effect/spell/item systems remain the owners of that.

### 2. Bard song cap → policy (`systems/spell-system.js`)
`applyBardMajorSongLimit` now reads the `major_song` cap from the policy via `enforceConcurrency` instead of the hard-coded `5`. **Behaviour-preserving**, with a fallback that keeps the old behaviour if the policy module is ever absent.

### 3. Forward-looking consumable categories
`food`, `potion_buff`, `potion_heal`, `potion_mana` (+ their cooldown groups) are pre-defined in the policy now as **data**, so the campfire cooking & alchemy work (Roadmap **Item 3**) consumes an existing policy instead of hard-coding fresh caps.
