# V0.20.88 — Waypoint Rebuilt Properly

Reported: *"Thats not even close to the waypoint image i gave you."*

That was right, and the more useful part of this report is **why I said it was done**.

## How I got it wrong

V0.20.86 was verified with a coarse **58×32 ASCII map** of the rendered output. It confirmed the dais,
both braziers, the rune ring, the core and the beam were all **present**, and I reported that as matching
the reference.

**Presence is not resemblance.** A 58×32 map cannot show proportion, saturation, or surface detail — and
those were precisely the three things wrong. The verification I ran could not have detected the failure
it was supposed to be checking for. That is worse than not checking, because it produced a confident
"verified" line in the report.

## The measured faults

Diagnosed by sampling the render, rather than guessing from the screenshot:

| fault | measurement |
|---|---|
| **far too small** | total footprint **75px wide**, against a ~150px-tall player sprite |
| **white blob** | centre saturated to **255,255,255 across a 12px band** at row 166 |
| **no masonry** | horizontal scan across the rim returned one flat grey |
| **no thickness** | a flat disc — no side wall at all |

The blob has a specific cause worth recording: the core was drawn with **source-over**
`rgba(238,250,255,0.95)`. That is white **paint** at 95% opacity, not light. It flattened everything
underneath it, which is why no ring, rune or stone detail survived in the middle.

## What changed

- **Radius 34 → 58.** Footprint **75 → 129px wide, 171px tall** — it now reads as a landmark standing
  beside a character rather than a token on the floor.
- **Real thickness.** The dais is drawn as a cylinder: a side wall with vertical joints below the top
  face. 15 rows of wall, measured.
- **Rim masonry.** 20 alternating fitted block faces with mortar lines. **16 distinct shades** measured
  around the arc, up from a single flat value.
- **Engraved courses** — three concentric cuts in the inner floor — plus **16 rune segments** that light
  in sequence when attuned.
- **All light is additive with capped alpha.** The glow blooms *over* the stone instead of erasing it,
  so the block work and the courses stay visible through the light.
- **Core is a highlight, not a smear.** A 4px disc inside a 20px bloom. Widest fully-saturated run
  through the centre: **8px**, down from 12.

## Verified against the four named faults

Not against "is it present" this time — against the specific things that were wrong:

| check | threshold | result |
|---|---|---|
| footprint width | ≥ 110px | **129** ✅ |
| no blown-out blob | widest centre saturated run ≤ 9px | **8** ✅ |
| masonry readable | ≥ 3 distinct rim shades | **16** ✅ |
| real thickness | ≥ 6 rows of side wall | **15** ✅ |

Remaining fully-saturated pixels: **138** across the two brazier flame cores and **17** at the beam base.
Those are hot cores in flames and a light source, which is where saturation belongs — confirmed by
clustering them by position rather than assuming.

## Lesson

When verifying something **visual**, the check has to be able to fail for the reason the thing would
actually look wrong. "Are the parts there" cannot catch "wrong size, blown out, no detail." Measuring
footprint, saturation width, shade variety and silhouette thickness can — and each of those maps directly
onto a way the image can look wrong.
