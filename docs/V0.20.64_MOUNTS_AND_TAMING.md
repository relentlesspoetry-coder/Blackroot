## B / C / D. The encounter and minigame

**Preparation** (all enforced, all tested): weaken below a per-beast health threshold, no other hostiles
within 7 tiles, correct bait in inventory, within 4.5 tiles, level and rank met, not swimming.

**The minigame** is deterministic and frame-driven — a seeded **mulberry32** PRNG, every transition
advanced by `update(dt)`, and **zero `setTimeout`/`setInterval`** anywhere (Item 7.J).

Four answers: **1 soothe · 2 hold · 3 feed · 4 give ground.** Each beast state telegraphs for a window,
then resolves. Difficulty compresses the window; rising trust widens it slightly, so a well-handled beast
becomes readable rather than infinitely punishing.

**Feints are the core idea.** 4 of the 6 patterns include false openings where *acting is the mistake and
doing nothing is correct.* Combined with agitation that drifts upward on its own — so freezing is not a
strategy either — the player has to actually read the animal.

Per-family behaviour, as the roadmap specifies: wolves bluff and test, boars charge and must be given
ground, spiders offer traps, stags bolt at anything sudden, crawlers telegraph hugely but punish hard.

## E. Outcomes

**Success:** unlocks the mount, records where and at what level it was tamed, removes the beast cleanly
from `enemies`/`entities` and clears targeting, auto-selects it if it is the first. Duplicate unlocks are
refused.

**Failure:** per-beast behaviour — `enrage` (turns on you), `flee`, or `untameable` — bait already spent,
plus a retry cooldown of 30–180s depending on the beast.

**Interrupts:** taking damage, moving more than 1.6 tiles, the beast being wounded, the beast dying,
distance > 6.5 tiles, changing zone, or pressing Escape.

## F / G / H / I. Mounts

**Collection panel (M):** each row draws the beast on **its own procedural model** — the same one the wild
creature uses, so a tamed beast looks exactly like the beast that was tamed, and locked entries show a
darkened silhouette. Shows family, rarity, speed bonus, traits, where/at what level it was tamed, and
what locked entries require. Select and favourite per mount. Rank shown Untrained → Master Handler.

**Controls (H):** mount/dismount.

**Safeguards — the important part.** Mount speed multiplies `Game.playerWalkSpeed()`, which is the single
funnel feeding `tryMoveActorSubstepped`. Everything downstream — collision, substepping, terrain, zone
boundaries — therefore applies unchanged. **A mount cannot cross anything the player could not walk
across**, which is exactly what Item 7.H demands and what a bespoke movement path would have broken.

Blocked in combat, in dungeons, in caves without `cave_sense`, in water without `swimmer`, while
meditating, mid-conversation. Continuous re-checks dismount if a condition arises while already mounted.
A hit can throw the rider, resisted by `dismountResist` (0.05 stag → 0.85 crawler).

**Traits are traversal-only** — no combat effects: forest stride, mud wading, sure-footed, swimmer, wall
climber, quiet step, cave sense.

## Persistence (Item 7.F, Item 10)

`serializeState`/`importState` through the normal character save, **and mirrored in
`resetCharacterOwnedState`** — so a new character on the same account cannot inherit a stable. That is the
V0.20.2 belongings-leak class of bug, and the mirror is the documented guard against it. `importState` is
called unconditionally on load so a pre-mounts save rebuilds an empty collection rather than keeping the
previous character's. A load always lands the player on foot.

## Two of my own bugs, caught in testing

1. **`session.zone` was read in `update()` but never assigned** — the change-zone interrupt was silently
   dead. It would have "worked" in every test that did not change zone.
2. **The reaction-window bar divided by `stateWindow`, which was never set**, so it rendered permanently
   full — the countdown communicated nothing.

Both fixed and re-verified (zone interrupt now fires; the bar ratio goes 1.00 → 0.86 over 0.2s).

Also worth recording: an early interrupt test appeared to fail because none of the attempts started. The
cause was **correct behaviour** — an enraged boar from the previous test was still aggro'd nearby, tripping
the "no other hostiles" precondition. The test setup was wrong, not the system.
