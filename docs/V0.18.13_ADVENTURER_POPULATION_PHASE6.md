# V0.18.13 — Adventurer Population, Phase 6 (death & recovery)

Sixth slice of the Persistent Adventurer Bot AI System (plan:
`docs/PLAN_ADVENTURER_BOT_AI.md`). Adventures now carry **real risk**: an
adventurer fighting at critical HP can fall, go to the spirit healer, take a
resurrection penalty, and recover.

## What Phase 6 delivers
- **Death roll (dormant records).** While an adventurer is actually at its site
  (not in town, not still travelling) and at **critical HP (≤ 15%)**, each in-game
  hour carries a chance of falling, scaled by the activity's danger.
- **Spirit healer + resurrection penalty.** On death: a lifetime death is recorded,
  the adventurer loses part of its level progress (XP) and a chunk of gold (the
  res/corpse cost), is sent to the spirit healer in town, and returns after a
  recovery window at reduced HP — then resumes adventuring.
- **Healer survivability.** Own healer role, a hired cleric, or a healer in the
  party both **softens HP loss** and **cuts the death chance** — so well-composed
  parties (and the mercs from Phase 5) are visibly safer.
- **Nobody is permanently lost.** Downed adventurers always recover (the spec's
  respawn), so the roster is stable over time.
- **Materialized adventurers** keep using the existing live-bot death/respawn flow;
  the abstract loop only governs off-screen records.

## Mechanics (`systems/adventurer-population-system.js`)
- `DEATH` tuning block (HP threshold, per-hour chance × danger, recovery hours,
  revive HP, XP/gold penalties, healer mitigation).
- Record fields: `deaths` (lifetime count), `reviveAtHour`, `hasHealerSupport`
  (recomputed each dawn from role/merc/party).
- The daily scheduler keeps `recovering` adventurers at the spirit healer (no
  activity/party/travel/spend) and computes healer support after merc hiring.
- The abstract sim revives them when `reviveAtHour` elapses, applies
  healer-mitigated HP loss, and runs the death roll.
- `/who` marks fallen adventurers with `†N`.

## Verified (in-browser, 0 console errors)
- **Death cycle:** a forced low-HP adventurer dies → `deaths` increments → moved to
  `recovering` with a revive time; XP and gold penalties apply (isolated with small
  time-steps); it stays at the healer through dawn (no adventuring); and revives to
  full HP when the window elapses.
- **Healer mitigation:** over 60 trials each, deaths dropped from **19 → 8** with
  healer support.
- **40-day continuous sim:** **21 deaths** across the four world adventurers, but
  **no permanent death** — everyone recovered. The pattern is emergent and correct:
  the squishy DPS ranger died most (12×), the self-healing cleric (3×) and tanky
  fighter (2×) least.

Rates are intentionally exposed in `DEATH`/`SIM` for the Phase 9 tuning pass; the
forced-arrival test maximizes death exposure, so real-play rates are lower.
