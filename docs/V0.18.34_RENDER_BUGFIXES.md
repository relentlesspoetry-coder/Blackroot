# V0.18.34 — Rendering bug fixes

Three reported rendering bugs (with screenshots), each fixed at the root cause in its owning Canvas module. No gameplay, data, or other systems were changed.

---

## Bug 1 — Rotling snare roots render at the player's waist, not the ground
**Report:** *"rotlings roots do not render at the ground level they render at the players waist. They need to be attached to the ground."*

**Root cause:** The rotling ("Snare Swarm") entangles the target and spawns a `rotlingRootEntangle` VFX drawn by `render/effects-renderer.js → drawRotlingRootEntangleEffect`. That effect IS anchored to the target's foot/ground anchor (`_rootVfxFootAnchor` / `_groundContactAnchor`), but its "wrapping tendrils" had been grown so tall (`topY = groundY − ~23`, motes at `groundY − 13`) that they climbed up to the hip/waist and stopped reading as ground roots.

**Fix:** Lowered the tendril and mote heights so the snare hugs the ankles/ground:
- `topY = groundY − (5 + (i%3)*2.2 + pulse*1.2)` (was `− (11 + (i%3)*4 + pulse*2)`)
- `midY = groundY − (1 + localGrow*5.5)` (was `− (2 + localGrow*12)`)
- mote band `groundY − 6` with a tighter vertical spread (was `groundY − 13`)

*(This bug's "roots on the player" appearance can also come from Bug 2 — a root/silkroot resource node on the player's tile drawn on top — which Bug 2's fix also resolves.)*

## Bug 2 — Herbs and other resources draw on top of players/bots/mobs/pets
**Report:** *"herbs and other resources should not render on top of players, bots, mobs or pets it should render as a ground layer."*

**Root cause:** Resource nodes were drawn by the resource system's `render(context)`, which runs in `renderExternalSystems(ctx)` — a **post-world overlay pass that executes after the depth-sorted entity/object pass** — so herbs/ore/roots painted on top of every actor.

**Fix (root cause, single owner):**
- `systems/resource-gathering-system.js`: renamed `render(context)` → `renderGroundLayer(context)`. Because `renderExternalSystems` only calls systems that have a `render` method, the resource system is no longer drawn as a post-world overlay.
- `game.js`: added `this.resourceGatheringSystem?.renderGroundLayer?.(ctx);` at the `layeredMapEditor.renderUnderEntities` hook — i.e. **after terrain, before the entity/object pass** — so resource props are a proper ground layer beneath the actors.
- Same `worldToScreen` coordinates under the same world-camera transform, so node positions are unchanged; only their z-order (draw layer) moved from "on top" to "ground layer."

## Bug 3 — The webbed corpse (silk cocoon) looks like trash
**Report:** *"The webbed corpse looks like trash, it doesn't look like spider webs at all, it doesn't even look like a body is covered in webs."*

**Root cause:** `render/object-renderer.js → drawSilkCocoon` (used by `silkCocoon` objects, incl. `cocoonType: 'corpse'`) drew a flat purple ellipse with a few horizontal ribs — reading as stripes, not silk over a body.

**Fix:** Rebuilt `drawSilkCocoon` to read as a body cocooned in spider silk:
- a tapered vertical pod (fattest at the body),
- a faint head/torso/hips silhouette showing through the silk,
- two families of **criss-crossing woven silk strands** (clipped to the pod),
- tapered **binding wraps** that cinch the silk (narrower at the ends),
- **frayed silk wisps** escaping the top,
- loose **web anchor threads** tying the cocoon to its surroundings.
- Deterministic per object position (seeded), so it doesn't shimmer/reshape while the camera pans. The `egg`/`poison`/`survivor`/`opened` variants are preserved.
