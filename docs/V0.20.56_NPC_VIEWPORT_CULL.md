# V0.20.56 — The Camp Was Being Drawn Off Screen

The first **measured and verified** frame-rate win of this run, found by the profiler added in V0.20.55.

## What the profiler said

External system **`npcRuntime`: 17–18ms/frame** — the largest single bucket, bigger than every entity in
the scene combined.

## Root cause

`systems/npc-system.js` culled with:

```js
if (Math.hypot(x + 0.5 - px, y + 0.5 - py) > 36) continue;
```

That is a **world radius, not a screen test**. Anywhere within 36 tiles of camp, every camp NPC counted
as "in range" — each paying for a full `mergedNpc()`, a full procedural class model and a full nameplate
**while completely off screen**.

## Why I missed it one version ago

I profiled **standing at spawn**, where 26 of 27 NPCs are genuinely on screen. The radius looked
harmless and I wrote: *"viewport culling would save almost nothing"* — with a real measurement behind it.

The screenshot that came back showed the player **out in the forest**, which is exactly the case where
that radius keeps a whole village rendering behind you. The measurement was correct; **the place I took
it was wrong**. Same failure family as testing the roots fix on a Fighter, and as measuring the splash
screen: a sample that cannot exhibit the bug.

## The fix

One call to the existing `game.worldPointInsideViewportFast(x, y, elev, 480)` — the same
cached-projection viewport test the entity renderer already uses — placed **after** the cheap radius
early-out but **before `mergedNpc()`**, so the expensive work is skipped rather than merely not blitted.

The 480px pad is far beyond the tallest NPC visual (~90px of model above the anchor plus a ~90px
nameplate stack), so nothing can pop in at the screen edge.

## Measured

Both arms **interleaved frame-by-frame** so drift hits them equally — the V0.20.55 lesson, where a tight
synthetic loop drifted 127ms → 148ms on identical settings and nearly produced another wrong answer.

| scenario | NPCs culled | `npcRuntime` median |
|---|---|---|
| **out in the forest** | **20 of 27** | **15.8ms → 4.2ms** |
| standing in camp | 0 of 27 | all 27 still drawn, no visual change |

The camp result is the regression check: where the NPCs really are visible, nothing is skipped.

## Caveat, stated plainly

The absolute milliseconds come from this preview, which renders in **software** and does not match real
hardware. What is machine-independent is the **work removed**: 20 of 27 full NPC draws, every frame,
whenever you are away from camp.

## Where to look next

From the same profile, still unexplained and worth checking on real hardware with
`await DarkWoodsGame.profileRenderPhases(120)`:

| phase | ms | calls |
|---|---|---|
| `drawEntity` | 15.8 | 17 |
| `drawObject` | 7.0 | 151 |
| `drawTile` | 3.3 | 58 |
| `drawNameplate` | 2.7 | 17 |

Also worth auditing: the same **world-radius-instead-of-viewport** pattern may exist in other render
paths. This one was worth ~12ms in the common case.
