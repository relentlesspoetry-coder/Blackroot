## The one genuine residual — and the fix

The V0.20.8 pass fixed the **exact** overlap (Quartermaster Brann had spawned on the Camp Merchant's
tile). But a proximity audit of the NPC layout found **two east-ring pairs still ~1 tile apart** — under
Item 2's own rule, "enough surrounding space for nameplates and interaction prompts to remain readable":

| pair | distance |
|---|---|
| `road_warden` (109,97) ↔ `fighter_trainer` (108,98) | 1.41 |
| `deepwood_surveyor` (109,103) ↔ `rogue_trainer` (109,104) | 1.00 |

Fix — each quest NPC moves **one tile** onto a tile chosen by a harness that required *prop-free +
walkable + ≥2.0 tiles from every other NPC*:

- `road_warden`: (109,97) → **(110,97)** — opens 1.41 → 2.24 from `fighter_trainer`
- `deepwood_surveyor`: (109,103) → **(109,102)** — opens 1.00 → 2.00 from `rogue_trainer`

No props were moved and no paths changed.

## An honest nuance the real-path check surfaced

Verifying through the live `editorNpcs` container (not just the source) showed `road_warden` carries a
`patrol: { mode: 'short_loop', radius: 2 }` behavior — it's a **patrolling guard**, so its instantaneous
spacing drifts by design (and the roadmap explicitly *wants* guards patrolling paths/entrances). The
**substantive, stable** fix is therefore `deepwood_surveyor` (a stationary NPC that genuinely sat 1.0
tile from a trainer, now a clean 2.0). `road_warden`'s anchor move still improves its baseline and does
no harm, but its spacing was always going to vary — worth stating plainly rather than implying a
patroller stands still.
