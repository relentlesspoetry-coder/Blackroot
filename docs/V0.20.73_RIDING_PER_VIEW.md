# V0.20.73 — Riding Pose Per Camera View

Reported: side view looks like the rider is sitting sideways; back view needs the mount's lower back and
tail over the rider's lower body.

## Root cause — the same parity bug, a third time

`buildRidingRig` chose its leg layout from `d.view`, which is **compass**-derived from `facingName`.
The mount overlay used `entityFacingView`, which is **camera-projected**.

They disagree. World `south` projects to a **side** view on this isometric screen. So heading south handed
the rider the wide *camera-facing* straddle **while side-on** — which is precisely the reported
"sitting sideways".

| heading | rig saw (compass) | screen actually shows | result |
|---|---|---|---|
| south | `front` → ±31 splay | **side** | legs out both ways = sideways ❌ |
| north | `back` → ±19 | **side** | legs out both ways ❌ |
| east / west | `side` | side | correct by luck ✅ |

**This is the third time this exact disagreement has caused a bug in the mount work:**

1. **V0.20.65** — which way the *animal* faced (wrong on 4 of 8 headings)
2. **V0.20.69** — whether the riding posture reached the model at all
3. **V0.20.73** — which seat the *rider* adopts

Fixed the same way each time, and now closed at the rig: `entity-renderer` publishes `e._cameraView`, and
`buildRidingRig` prefers it over `d.view`.

## Side view — one leg

Both legs sit on the **near** side:

| leg | knee | foot |
|---|---|---|
| near | `nearSign * 14` | `nearSign * 16` |
| far | `nearSign * 7`, 3px higher | `nearSign * 8`, 4px higher |

It reads as one visible leg with the far one glimpsed past the barrel, rather than legs sticking out
both ways.

## Rear view — the rump laps the rider

`mountFrontHeadBox` generalised into `mountOverlayBox(def, scale, mode)`:

- **`front`** — measures the **head**: top mass down to where the outline widens at the shoulders
- **`back`** — measures the **rump**: top of the back through the upper third, kept deliberately shallow
  so it laps the pelvis without burying the legs (the report said those already looked right)

The overlay redraws that region after the rider for front *and* back. Side-on, nothing is nearer the
camera than the rider, so it returns early.

## Verified across 6 headings

| heading | camera view | leg feet X | both same side | overlay drew |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| east | side | 8, 16 | ✅ | no |
| west | side | −16, −8 | ✅ | no |
| **south** | side | 8, 16 | ✅ | no |

| southeast | front | −31, 31 | — | **yes (head)** |
| northwest | back | −19, 19 | — | **yes (rump)** |

`south` and `north` previously got the wide front straddle; they now correctly resolve to side.
