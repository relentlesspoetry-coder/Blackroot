# V0.18.15 — Adventurer Population, Phase 8 (world happenings & social)

Eighth slice of the Persistent Adventurer Bot AI System (plan:
`docs/PLAN_ADVENTURER_BOT_AI.md`). The world now throws **happenings** the
adventurers respond to, plus a light social touch.

## What Phase 8 delivers
- **Dynamic world events.** There's no dedicated world-boss/caravan system to hook,
  so this adds a lightweight abstract one: **one happening runs at a time**, spaced
  out on the in-game clock, with a location, a duration, and a type:
  - **Merchant caravan** — trade profit (high gold), open to all levels;
  - **Rumored beast** — a dangerous, glory-rich hunt (high rep + risk), level 6+;
  - **Call to arms** — defend near the camp (rep), level 4+.
- **Adventurers answer the call.** At the next dawn, eligible adventurers (of
  fitting level) drop their usual plans, pay for supplies, and travel to the event —
  and the abstract sim rewards them there (a caravan pads their purse, a beast hunt
  builds renown but can get them killed). When the event passes, they return to
  normal life.
- **Social colour.** When several adventurers spend the day in town, they gather —
  an occasional "adventurers rest and swap stories around the campfire" line.

## Mechanics (`systems/adventurer-population-system.js`)
- `WORLD_EVENTS` (caravan/beast/call_to_arms with reach, duration, and sim rates)
  and derived `EVENT_ACTIVITIES`; `EVENT_RESPONSE_CHANCE`.
- `updateAdventurerWorldEvents(dt)` — starts/ends the single active event on the
  in-game clock (announcing both), placing it at a walkable point via
  `findBotSpawnPoint`; hooked into the frame update **before** the daily scheduler
  so the dawn roll sees it.
- The dawn roll adds an **event-response branch** (level-gated, `EVENT_RESPONSE_CHANCE`),
  routing responders to the event and tracking them; the sim handles event
  activities through `EVENT_ACTIVITIES` (rewards + danger, including death rolls for
  the beast hunt).
- `adventurerWorldEventInfo()` for inspection; a campfire social line in the roll.
