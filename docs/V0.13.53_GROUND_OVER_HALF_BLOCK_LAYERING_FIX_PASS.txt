Dream Realms V0.13.53 — Ground Over Half-Block Layering Fix Pass

Systems changed:
- game.js
  - Split cached-ground structural rendering into a dedicated terrain-depth-underlay phase, terrain-surface phase, terrain-occlusion-cap phase, and unchanged structural wall/object/actor phases.
  - Ground elevation faces that belong to cached walkable terrain now render before cached water/ground surfaces instead of after them.
  - Cached terrain fallback remains intact: if chunk surfaces are unavailable, affected tiles return to the full legacy draw path.
- render/terrain-renderer.js
  - Added structural draw modes for depth-underlay and occlusion-cap rendering inside the owning terrain renderer.
  - Reused the existing elevation-face renderer for the lower/depth wall face while suppressing its old post-surface ledge lip in the underlay pass.
  - Added a systematic material-colored ground surface occlusion lip that draws after cached ground surfaces and hides the upper seam of half-block/depth faces.
  - Added explicit neighboring tile sampling metadata for terrain chunk signatures so edge masks and lips invalidate across chunk boundaries.
- core/config.js, data/default-zones.js, systems/save-system.js, systems/world-serializer.js, README.txt, VERSION.txt, and Dream Realms V0.13.53.html
  - Updated build/version metadata from V0.13.52 to V0.13.53.
- docs/MODULAR_HISTORY.txt
  - Added this pass at the top of modular history.

Terrain layer-order fix:
- Previous cached terrain order drew water/ground surfaces first, then emitted half-block elevation faces through the structural tile path.
- V0.13.53 changes the visual order to:
  1. water depth faces
  2. cached-ground terrain depth underlay faces
  3. cached water surfaces and shimmer
  4. cached walkable ground surfaces
  5. material ground occlusion lips/caps
  6. cached shoreline caps
  7. structural cave walls, non-cached tiles, props, actors, effects, labels, and UI overlays
- This makes the walkable top surface visually sit above the half-block/depth wall face.

Half-block/depth-face occlusion fix:
- Half-block vertical faces now render as lower terrain depth geometry beneath cached walkable ground.
- The old ledge lip is not drawn during the depth-underlay pass, preventing it from sitting on top of the world surface.
- A material-colored ground occlusion lip is drawn afterward from the owning terrain renderer, using the tile’s ground material instead of a generic dark box or hard outline.
- The cap covers the upper seam of the depth face while preserving the recessed cliff/drop-off read.

Chunk composition changes:
- The terrain chunk cache remains the owner of cached water, cached ground, shimmer, material transitions, and shoreline caps.
- Chunk composition order is now documented in runtime cache stats as: water-depth, terrain-depth-underlay, water-surface, ground-surface, ground-occlusion-cap, shore-cap, structural-walls, actors-props.
- Terrain chunk signatures now sample two tiles beyond chunk bounds for edge-dependent invalidation, while the existing three-tile build padding remains in place.
- No full-map redraw, duplicate render loop, monkey patch, bottom-of-file hotfix, or hardcoded coordinate exception was added.

Gameplay systems preserved:
- Logical tile types, elevation values, collision, water blocking, pathfinding, spawn validation, quest/objective placement, loot placement, combat distance, ranged auto attacks, Purify, resurrection, autonomous bots, pets, dungeon logic, paper doll, bag-to-ground drag/drop, item stat compare, currency drops, save/load, and Esc menu behavior remain unchanged.
- The fix is limited to terrain render ordering, terrain chunk signature sampling, and material seam caps.
