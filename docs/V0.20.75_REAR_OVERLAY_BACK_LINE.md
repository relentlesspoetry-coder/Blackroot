# V0.20.75 — The Rear Overlay Anchors to the Back Line

Reported: *"the back view shouldn't show the head like that."* The screenshot showed the black wolf's
head — ears, muzzle and all — drawn across the rider's chest and tabard.

## Root cause

`mountOverlayBox`'s rear branch measured its band downward from `top`, the topmost row of the silhouette,
on the assumption that from behind the near mass is the rump.

**The topmost mass is the head from every angle, including from behind.**

Measured rear profile, black wolf:

| dy | width | |
|---|---|---|
| −84 → −52 | 21 → 44 | the skull |
| **−48** | **71** | shoulders / back begins |
| +8 | 89 | haunches (widest) |

The box covered **−90..−49** — the head, almost exactly. The V0.20.73 comment called it "the rump: from
the top of the back downward", which is where the error hid: the *name* was right and the *measurement*
was of something else entirely.

## The fix

Anchor to the **back line**: the first row where the outline widens to `maxW * 0.6`. The band cannot begin
on skull or neck by construction.

Then clamp it to the pelvis:

```js
backY = Math.max(backY, Math.round(oy - this.mountSaddleHeight(def, modelScale)) - 3);
```

Depth is a flat **14px**. The rider's foot sits only ~20px below the back line, and the report said the
legs already looked right — so the haunch laps thigh and knee while the shin and foot hang clear. A null
guard covers the case where the band catches no pixels, so a garbage clip rect can never reach `clip()`.

## Two wrong turns, both discarded on evidence

Recording these because in both cases the reasoning was plausible and the measurement disagreed.

### 1. Anchoring to the saddle height

Appealing: the saddle is already measured per beast and is by definition where the rider sits. But the
saddle is measured from a **side** view, and at that height a **rear** silhouette is still neck and skull.

Measured result: box at **−64..−40** against a head at **−84..−52** — a 12px overlap onto the skull base.
It would have reintroduced the exact reported bug. Rejected.

### 2. Restricting the width measurement to the lower half

To stop antlers skewing `maxW`, I measured body width from the lower half only, wrote a comment stating
this was what fixed the stags — and then tested it.

**It is not.** `bodyMax` and `wholeMax` select the same anchor row on **all 11 mounts**:

| mount | bodyMax | wholeMax | anchor differs |
|---|---|---|---|
| hollow stag | 119 | 119 | no |
| thorn-crowned stag | 148 | 148 | no |
| cave bat | 76 | 95 | **no** (same row regardless) |

A stag's antlers are not wider than its ribcage in a rear silhouette. The restriction was dead complexity
and the comment was a false explanation; both were removed.

**What actually corrects the stags and arachnids is the pelvis clamp.** On both stags and all three
arachnids the back line sits *above* the rider's pelvis, so the band landed across the **chest** — the
wrong half of the body.

| mount | back line | rider hip | band before clamp | after |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| hollow stag | −111 | −82 | −112..−94 (chest) | **−90..−72** |
| thorn widow | −39 | −18 | −40..−22 (chest) | **−26..−8** |

## Verified — all 11 mounts

| property | result |
|---|---|
| box starts at or below the back line | **11/11** |
| box contains real body pixels (≥ 50% body width) | **11/11** |
| laps hip-to-knee | **11/11** |
| spares the foot | **11/11** |
| spares the chest | **11/11** |

**Negative control:** re-injecting the old top-anchored rule **fails** the check — wolf −89 and stag −133,
both above their back lines. The check has teeth; it is not vacuously true.

## Known limitation — unchanged from V0.20.74

**I still cannot pixel-verify the finished image.** `worldToScreen()` does not return the same coordinate
space `drawEntity` draws in — the world camera transform is applied *inside* the draw path. Everything
above is silhouette geometry measured off an offscreen probe canvas, which is sound, but it is not a
screenshot of the result.

The documented route to real pixel verification remains: capture the transformed `foot` from *inside*
`drawEntity` (wrap `drawRiddenMount` and record its argument) rather than recomputing position outside the
transform.
